DROWSINESS DETECTION

Whether it’s due to medication, a sleep disorder or a poor night’s rest, new research points to the risks and potential dangers of drowsy driving. Missing one to two hours of the recommended seven hours of sleep a night nearly doubles the risk of a car accident.Sleepiness can come without warning, so drivers should prioritize getting enough sleep and avoid driving when they are fatigued.

The best drivers are aware that they must be beware.

Solutions for Drivers Facing Fatigue or Drowsiness

1. Frequent Breaks

Stop and rest every two hours, especially during long drives. A short break can refresh your focus and energy levels.

2. Stay Hydrated

Dehydration can increase feelings of fatigue. Drink water regularly to stay alert.

3. Power Naps

Take a 15-30 minute nap if you're feeling extremely tired. It can significantly improve alertness.

4. Caffeine Boost

A cup of coffee or an energy drink can help, but don’t rely solely on caffeine for alertness.

5. Eat Light Snacks

Opt for light, healthy snacks like fruits, nuts, or granola bars to maintain your energy levels.

6. Use the Drowsiness Detection System

Enable our system to alert you when signs of drowsiness appear and take action promptly.

7. Keep the Car Ventilated

Keep the windows open slightly or use air conditioning to allow fresh air in and avoid feeling drowsy.

8. Listen to Engaging Podcasts

Listening to upbeat music or an engaging podcast helps to keep you mentally engaged and alert.

9. Avoid Driving During Peak Sleep Hours

Try to avoid driving between midnight and 6 a.m. or during the afternoon slump, when drowsiness is more likely.

10. Use Companion Systems

Take turns driving or talk with someone to stay focused and avoid fatigue.
